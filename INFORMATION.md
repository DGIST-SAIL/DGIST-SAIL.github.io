**Detailed Bio (from Self Intro):**
"I am deeply interested in developing wearable robotic technologies that enhance human functional movement and support rehabilitation. In recent years, wearable robots—including exoskeletons and exosuits—have demonstrated significant progress in augmenting and improving human motion through advanced engineering technologies. However, for these robots to be naturally and effectively used in everyday settings, they must be capable of perceiving their environment and accurately recognizing the user’s intentions to provide personalized control.

Looking ahead, I believe we are entering an era in which humans will collaborate—or even compete—with AI-driven robots. However, I believe that judgment, creative problem-solving, and social coordination remain uniquely human strengths. I hold a firm conviction that technology should not replace humanity, but rather amplify and enhance its value. Guided by this belief, I seek to continue my research in robotics and AI-driven wearable technologies to expand human potential as a faculty member at DGIST."

## 2. Research
**Lab Theme:** SAIL — Soft Augmented Intelligence Lab (소프트 증강지능 연구실)

### Research Interests
1.  **Force Estimation and Control**: Deep learning-based force estimation for soft grippers and wearable robots without external sensors.
2.  **Vision-Based Intention Recognition**: Real-time rehabilitation assistance systems using deep learning to recognize hand motion intentions.
3.  **Simulation-to-Real Integrated Control**: Leveraging physics-based simulation data combined with real-world measurements for robust control.

### Key Publications (Abstracts)

**1. Dissertation: Deep Learning-Based Grip Force Estimation & Intention Recognition**
*Summary:* Addresses limitations in soft wearable robotic gloves by using deep learning to estimate grip force on soft objects and recognize user intention via vision, enabling seamless interaction and rehabilitation.

**2. Impact of Physical Parameters and Vision Data on Deep Learning-Based Grip Force Estimation for Fluidic Origami Soft Grippers**
*Summary:* Presents "OriGripNet", a vision-based neural network that estimates gripping force by combining RGB image data with physical parameters of a soft gripper, achieving high accuracy without embedded sensors.

**3. Learning Fingertip Force to Grasp Deformable Objects for Soft Wearable Robotic Glove With TSM**
*Summary:* Proposes a deep-learning model using LSTM to estimate fingertip forces on deformable objects by estimating object stiffness, improving accuracy by 45% compared to standard methods.

**4. Multiple Hand Posture Rehabilitation System Using Vision-Based Intention Detection and Soft-Robotic Glove**
*Summary:* A rehabilitation system comprising a vision-based intention detection framework and an 8-DoF soft-robotic glove to help stroke survivors train multiple hand postures.

**5. Separation-free bacterial identification in arbitrary media via deep neural network-based SERS analysis**
*Summary:* Demonstrates "DualWKNet" for rapid, separation-free bacterial detection using SERS and deep learning, achieving up to 98% accuracy.
